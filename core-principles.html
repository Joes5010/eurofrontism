Core Principles

Here are the core principles of Eurofrontism:

  • Preserving National Essence within European Unity: In a united federal Europe, the unique identity of each nation — its people, languages, history, heritage, customs, and all that defines it — will be protected and kept alive. While political sovereignty unites under one European banner, the soul of every country will remain untouched, forming the living fabric of a shared European civilization. .
  • Unified Defense and Common Purpose: Advocating for a united Europe capable of defending itself against external threats, with member states standing together under one banner while honoring their unique histories and people.
  • Preservation of National Identity: While united under a single European state, each nation's language, history, heritage, traditions, and people will be respected and preserved — ensuring the soul of every country remains alive within the greater European civilization.
  • Socially-Oriented Governance: Support for government policies that prioritize the well-being of the people, incorporating socialist ideas in terms of social safety nets and ensuring the general welfare of the citizenry.
  • Strong and Respected Government: Advocacy for a capable and respected government that can maintain order, enforce laws, and command the respect of all citizens, including the youth through fostering discipline.
  • Controlled and Legal Migration: Acceptance of legal immigration processes while opposing mass and illegal immigration, with a focus on maintaining social cohesion and national identity.
  • Rejection of Extremism: Opposition to all forms of political extremism, recognizing the importance of balance and reasoned discourse.
  • Rejection of Marxist Socialism and Capitalism (In terms of Class Conflict): Both systems, despite their opposing ideologies, fail to resolve class conflict—Marxist socialism often exchanges one hierarchy for another, while capitalism exploits workers for profit..
  • Rejection of Communism and Fascism: Clear opposition to both communist and fascist political systems.
